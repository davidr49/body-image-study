About the Body Image and Ethnicity Study

This study is exploring the role of ethnicity and culture in body dissatisfaction and body image.

The study also wants to explore how these types of difficulties (i.e. eating disorders and body dysmorphic disorder) could be prevented in the future.

Please find the sponsor sitehere.

To do this, there will be a series of online focus groups held with young people across England, to explore how they have experienced body image concerns. I am conducting this research because at the moment, the Department for Education and the Department for Health and Social Care have made proposals to introduce body image and eating disorder groups for adolescent girls in schools, which aim to challenge something called the ‘Appearance Ideal’. This is great, but I want to make sure that the type of activity they are proposing is appropriate and helpful for all girls, and inclusive of different ethnicities, cultures and religions. To do this, I want to talk to people who have the first-hand experience of body image problems and use their valuable insights and expertise. Although the results of this particular project may not directly influence the government, this project will contribute to my wider PhD study and the research base for school-based interventions.

The study is important because it could influence prevention strategies in the school environment which could stop young people from developing problems associated with body image such as eating disorders and body dysmorphic disorder.
